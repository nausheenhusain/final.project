Data Viz Final Project Pitch: Chicago Public Schools "Safe Passage Routes"


The Pitch

CPS has deemed certain sections of streets "safe passage routes" for kids in the city of Chicago who have to walk to school. Many publications have reported that the safe passage routes aren't actually all that safe. I'd like to map where exactly the safe passage routes are with how much crime there has actually been on those blocks.
The map would be interactive so that a reader can click on a CPS school and see crime that has taken place within a few blocks of that school.

News Angle

CPS decided to close almost 50 CPS schools over the summer because of budgetary reasons. Critics of that decision have complained that students will now have to walk to their new schools through dangerous areas of Chicago, particularly in the South Side. CPS has designated areas around CPS schools "safe passage routes" and are working to reduce crime in those particular areas.

Sources

Chicago Public Schools Chicago Police Department

Background

The Sun-Times has been covering crime on safe passage routes since at least the beginning of the summer, and the New York Times has a map that I think I might be able to use for my project.

Publication

I would like to pitch this to the Chicago Sun-Times.

Preliminary Questions

How much crime has happened along "safe passage routes" since it began in 2009? CPS claims that there has been a 20 percent decrease in criminal incidents along safe passage schools - is this true? Has there been a long-term decline? Have crime rates increased or decreased significantly since the closing of almost 50 CPS schools in June?
